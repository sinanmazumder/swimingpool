Sizing your pool for the right heater r

Finding the surface area for your pool size and calculating the right amount of BTUs necessary to heat your water may seem a bit daunting. Do not worry we will try and make it easy to understand so you can find the right heater unit for your pool size. There is a lot of information that goes into calculating the amount of BTUs needed to raise your pool water temperature by just one degree Fahrenheit. We don't want to confuse you so simply put it takes 8.34 BTUs to raise one gallon of water by 1 degree Fahrenheit.


To calculate the amount of gallons in your pool we need to figure out the surface area, then we'll know which pool heater you need with minimum BTU. First multiply the length of your pool by the width of your pool.( It is ok if you are slightly over with these numbers, it could be a good thing). Then multiply your surface area by six. (6 feet is the average depth of most residential pools). If you have a diving board chances are you have a deeper pool, so multiply your surface area by 12, not 6. For this example we'll be using 6 for the pool depth since most homeowners have a pool that is six feet deep. After multiplying the surface area by 6, you should have the total amount of volume in cubic feet. For example, if you have a pool with a length of 25 feet and a width of 15 feet your calculation would look like this; (25×15) x 6 = 16830 cubic feet. After you have the amount of volume in cubic feet ( the 16830 ) multiple it by 7.48, because 7.48 gallons of water equals 1 cubic foot. Once you multiply your total cubic feet by 7.48, you'll have the total estimated amount of gallons for your pool.


Next we need to figure out the temperature difference from the current water temperature to your desired temperature. So if your pool is 55 degrees but you want your pool water to be 75 degrees subtract the two and you'll have the difference (75-55=20). In this example we need to raise the water temperature by 20 degrees Fahrenheit. It takes 8.34 BTUs to raise the water temperature of one gallon by 1 degree. So we need to multiply the difference in temperature by 8.34 to simplify the following equation (20×8.34=166.8). We know we need 166.8 BTUs per gallon of water to raise it by 20 degrees. Don't worry, the best pool heater for your pool is only a few sentences and equations away, hang in there!


Since we have 53846 gallons of water, we need to multiply the total amount of gallons by 166.8 to get the minimum amount of BTUs needed to raise the pool temperature by 20 degrees (53846 x 166.8 = 8981512.8 BTUs). There isn't a pool heater that will produce 8981512.9 BTUs so we will allow the heater unit time to raise the pool temperature by 20 degrees, let's give it 48 hours. This part is easy. Divided the total amount of BTUs needed by 48 (8981512.8 / 48 = 187114.85 BTUs). Great! Now we know it will take a minimum of 187114.85 BTUs to raise the temperature of this pool by 20 degrees, in 48 hours.if you need more assistance we can help find the right heater depending on your pool size.
